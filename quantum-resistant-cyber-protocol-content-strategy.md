### Lattice-Based Cryptography

**What It Is:**
Lattice-based cryptography derives its security from the computational difficulty of problems involving high-dimensional geometric structures called lattices. Finding the shortest vector or closest point in these complex mathematical spaces remains infeasible even for quantum computers.

**Why It Matters:**
Lattice problems have been studied by mathematicians for decades without finding efficient quantum solutions. This extensive academic scrutiny provides confidence in their long-term security.

**Our Implementation:**
- **CRYSTALS-Kyber (ML-KEM)**: Our primary key encapsulation mechanism for secure key exchange
- **CRYSTALS-Dilithium (ML-DSA)**: Our standard digital signature algorithm for authentication
- **FALCON (FN-DSA)**: Our lightweight signature solution for resource-constrained devices

**Business Benefits:**
- Proven security against both classical and quantum attacks
- Efficient performance suitable for real-world applications
- Supports advanced features like homomorphic encryption for privacy-preserving computation

---

### Hash-Based Cryptography

**What It Is:**
Hash-based signatures rely on the security of cryptographic hash functionsâ€”one-way mathematical operations that produce unique fingerprints of data. Breaking these requires finding collisions or preimages, problems that quantum computers don't solve efficiently.

**Why It Matters:**
Hash functions like SHA-256 and SHA-3 are among the most thoroughly analyzed components in modern cryptography. Their security is well-understood and widely trusted.

**Our Implementation:**
- **SPHINCS+ (SLH-DSA)**: Our stateless hash-based signature scheme providing additional algorithmic diversity

**Business Benefits:**
- Conservative security approach based on well-established primitives
- Stateless operation simplifies key management
- Provides hedge against potential advances in lattice cryptanalysis

---

### Multivariate Cryptography

**What It Is:**
Multivariate cryptography bases its security on the difficulty of solving systems of multivariate polynomial equations over finite fields. These problems are NP-hard, meaning no efficient algorithmâ€”quantum or classicalâ€”is known to solve them.

**Why It Matters:**
Multivariate schemes provide a fundamentally different mathematical foundation than lattice-based approaches, offering valuable diversity in your cryptographic portfolio.

**Our Implementation:**
- **Rainbow**: Our multivariate signature scheme for specialized applications

**Business Benefits:**
- Algorithmic diversity reduces systemic risk
- Efficient signature generation and verification
- Compact signature sizes for bandwidth-constrained environments

---

### Code-Based Cryptography

**What It Is:**
Code-based cryptography uses error-correcting codes to create encryption schemes. Breaking these schemes requires solving the general decoding problem, which remains computationally infeasible for quantum computers.

**Why It Matters:**
Code-based cryptography has been studied since the 1970s, providing decades of cryptanalytic scrutiny and confidence in its security.

**Our Implementation:**
- **Classic McEliece**: Our code-based encryption option for maximum long-term security

**Business Benefits:**
- Among the most conservative post-quantum options
- Extremely fast encryption and decryption
- Well-understood security characteristics
